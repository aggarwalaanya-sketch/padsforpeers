Pads for Peers

Breaking barriers. Ending period poverty.

Period poverty affects 1 in 10 girls every month. In the United States alone, almost 17 million people who need them lack access to menstrual hygiene products such as sanitary pads and tampons. Menstrual products cost at least $10 per month per person, which can be too expensive for many people. This lack of resources can lead to some young girls missing one or more days of school every month, affecting their education, health, and self-confidence.

Our Mission

Our mission is to make comfortable and good-quality menstrual hygiene products available and accessible to students who need them and to cultivate a supportive and open culture around periods in these schools.

Who We Are

Our Mission

Our Story

We are two high schoolers from the Bay Area, Aanya Aggarwal and Laya Sunkara, who started Pads for Peers in 2023 because we experienced firsthand the stigma and lack of knowledge surrounding menstruation in our society. On top of all that, upon noticing the unmet need for period products in our area, we felt that the issue required immediate action.

In 8th grade, for one of our classes, we were assigned with identifying an issue in society and how we, as students, could work towards solving it. Since then, after months of researching period poverty and its effects on the student population, we have been building Pads for Peers, which aims to donate menstrual products to underprivileged Bay Area schools in an effort to combat period poverty amongst students.

What We Do

Comfort Box Program

Our Comfort Box is a customizable monthly subscription box that lets participants choose the period products they need. We provide free products while keeping everything confidential through a secure QR code system for discreet pickup and delivery. Currently, over 50 students at De Anza College receive Comfort Boxes, and we're working to expand the program's impact.

Providing Period Education

Breaking the stigma and misinformation around menstruation starts with education. Many young students don't fully understand what it means to have a period, so we focus on open, discussion-based conversations rather than traditional classroom instruction. This approach helps students feel more comfortable sharing, asking questions, and learning about menstrual health.

Why Us

Though there are many period poverty-related nonprofits, donating to Pads for Peers makes a significant impact on small communities, particularly within the vulnerable student population, who are often not focused on. Young people are at a critical stage in their lives where their experiences and perceptions about menstruation will shape their lifelong attitudes and behaviors.

By supporting Pads for Peers, you are directly contributing to a cause that ensures students don't miss school due to lack of menstrual products and helping to destigmatize periods for future generations. Every dollar you donate goes straight to providing essential products and education to those who need it most, fostering a more informed and supportive environment for young people.

Period Poverty Info

Period poverty forces millions to endure menstruation without proper products, resorting to unsafe alternatives like rags or paper. This leads to infections, severe health risks, and intense shame. Many people miss school or work because of this, which worsens poverty and deepens gender inequality. The lack of access to menstrual products perpetuates stigma and discrimination, highlighting the urgent need for action to ensure everyone can manage their periods with dignity and safety.
